Mortred has had a misreported HP in every FAQ I've read. He actually starts with approximately 1000 HP. The reason that it is misreported, is that he silently gains 100 HP (fixed) per round, with a maximum of 1000 HP (fixed). This is why the reported numbers of his HP wildly vary. If you simply add it, it will be based on the amount of rounds it takes you to kill him. I've yet to take his helmet off, so I'm not sure if it continues past that.

Also, Sleep starts at an enemy at 4, and will count down to zero. They have a probabilistic chance of waking up before then, but will definitely wake up once the counter reaches zero. The counter decreases every time you see "Enemy is asleep!" Casting Sleep while the counter is > 0 will reset the counter to 4.

There are 3 mandatory (i.e. non-probabilistic, the RNG can't prevent the encounter) that occur in the game (or, at least that I've found so far), that no one lists as a boss. Two of these can be avoided by walking backwards.
#1 is the Sea Stallion in the first Pool in the Cave of Truth. Walking backwards through the pool will avoid this conflict.
#2 is the Head-hunter that drops through the ceiling in Floor 2. Also can be avoided by traversing the hallway backwards
#3 is the Kromeball in Floor 2, right before the stairs. No known way to avoid this conflict.

Whether or not Gila will attack is decided BEFORE the round starts, (i.e. before you get the chance to say fight/magic/etc.) This is of little consequence to anyone not trying to control the RNG, but may still be interesting.

It turns out - Criticals "Awesome Blow" are not effected by the luck stat.
It is actually effected by the Character, and the Weapon the character equips.
All start at a value of 8 - this is listed in 0xFF151E for Hero, 0xFF1520 for Milo, and 0xFF1522 for Pyra.
For example, the Light blade value increases this by 25.
The way to calculate the actual percentage is to take the number, and divide by 256.
Thus, the starting critical rate is 3%. With the light blade (33/256) = 12.8%
I could probably come up with a table that says how much each weapon/armor affects this.
